Telegram is a secure messaging and audio calling cloud-based app that allows you to send messages, photos, videos and files of any type to your contacts for free. You can also create groups for up to 200,000 people or channels for broadcasting to unlimited contacts or audiences. Telegram is designed so that the content you share is private, secure and encrypted, allowing you to have 'secret chats' and for the messages you send or receive to 'self-destruct' so they can no longer be seen by the sender or the recipient.

Telegram uses a variety of privacy technologies including encryption to make your communication secure. It also uses cloud technology to store data and synchronise accounts across multiple devices. It supports most file types that are 1.5GB or less and places no limits on the amount of content being sent.

Creating an account on Telegram is free with a phone number, which is only visible to those already in your contact list. You can connect with other people on Telegram by searching for their username or sending invitation links.

Telegram also uses 'bots' (small applications which run inside the app) and provides support for developers wanting to create their own.

## How do people use Telegram?

Telegram is used for: encrypted messaging and online chat, money transfers and online payments, online relationships, photo and video sharing, screen capture and voice chat.

## Privacy and security

One of Telegram's key selling points is its emphasis on privacy. All messages are encrypted in transit, and Secret Chats use end-to-end encryption. Messages in Secret Chats can be set to self-destruct after a specified time.

However, it is worth noting that regular cloud chats are encrypted between your device and Telegram's servers, but Telegram itself holds the keys — meaning it could, in theory, access those messages. Only Secret Chats offer true end-to-end encryption where only the two parties in the conversation can read the messages.

## Channels and communities

Telegram channels allow one-way broadcasting to an unlimited number of subscribers — making them popular for news outlets, content creators, and organisations who want to reach large audiences without the algorithmic friction of social media platforms.

Groups can accommodate up to 200,000 members and include features like polls, pinned messages, and topic threads, making them viable as community spaces for everything from hobby groups to activist networks.

## Concerns and controversies

Telegram's permissive moderation policies have made it a home for both legitimate privacy advocates and bad actors. The platform has faced criticism for hosting extremist content, coordinating harassment campaigns, and being used for the sale of illegal goods.

The arrest of founder Pavel Durov in France in 2024 brought renewed scrutiny to the platform's content moderation practices and the legal responsibilities of encrypted messaging services.

These tensions — between privacy and accountability, between open communication and harm reduction — are not unique to Telegram. They are the defining challenges of the encrypted internet.
