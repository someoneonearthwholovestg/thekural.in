In a world optimised for speed, there is a quiet rebellion in choosing to think slowly — to sit with an idea until it fully blooms.

We have built tools that compress time: instant messages, one-click answers, summaries of summaries. Somewhere in that compression, depth got left behind.

## What slow thinking actually means

Slow thinking isn't laziness. It is the deliberate act of resisting the first, loudest answer. It is the practice of holding a question open — like a window — and letting air move through it before you close it into a conclusion.

The psychologist Daniel Kahneman called this *System 2 thinking*: effortful, deliberate, and often uncomfortable. It requires you to stay in uncertainty a little longer than feels natural.
